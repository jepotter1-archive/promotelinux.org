Linux

A free alternative to Windows and macOS

Free of Malware

Is your computer running slowly or acting strange? Approximately 30% of U.S. computers are infected with malware — malicious programs that infiltrate your computer and can display intrusive ads, spy on you, lock up your personal files or steal your passwords. Linux is completely free of malware, so you never have to pay for antivirus or worry about being infected again.

Sources: AV-Test 2015/16 Security Report, InfoWorld

Free & Open Source

Linux is Free/Libre and Open Source Software. Windows and Mac are run by large companies whose sole purpose is to make a profit off you and force you to sign restrictive legal contracts, like End User License Agreements (EULA) and Terms of Service (ToS), that strip your rights away. Linux is a community project created by other users who just want a better experience on their computer, and is licensed using the GNU Public License, which allows you to do pretty much anything with the software, so you can see what it's actually doing, how it works and modify it to suit your needs. You can even make your own copy and put it on the internet.

Plus, Linux is free, now and always, no catches. And you'll get all future updates, no matter how old your computer is.
